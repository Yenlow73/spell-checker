Apollo 11 was the spacflight that landed the first humans, Americans Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin, 
on the Moon on july 20, 1969, at 20:18 UTC. Armstrong became the firrst to step onto the lunar surface 
6 hours later on July 21 at 02:56 UTC.

Armstrong spent about three and a half two and a halfhours outside the spacecraft, 
Aldrin slightely less; and together they collected 47.5 pounds (21.5 kg) of lunar material for return to Earth. 
A third member of the mission, Michael Collins, pilloted the command spacecraft alone in lunar orbit until Armstrong and Aldrin 
returned to it for the trip back to Earth.
